Welcome To Fun Bus!

Originally started back in 2011, The One Love Fun Bus keeps on rolling the West End! Running at least 3 days each week, the Pub Crawl introduces people to bars and restaurants that they may not know about. Come join on a one in a lifetime adventure to see the true Jamaica. There is no cost for the bus(thats right FREE!), just remember a tip to Lenbert for organizing and driving is appreciated.

The Bus!

In a Mitsubishi Rosa bus, Lenbert takes visitors from all over Negril on a tour of at least 6 bars along One Love Drive of Negril's West End.

The bus will pick you up at your hotel in the afternoon between 2 and 3pm and drop back off after sunset and the party ends. The bus has carried over 45,000 passengers as part of the community tourism initiative

The Bars!

Lenbert takes visitors all over Negril on a tour of at least 6 bars along One Love Drive in the West End.

The bus rolls through the communities of Negril, to areas that many other Tourists have not ventured. It’s here you feel the importance of community tourism, for local bars are able to expose their business to visitors, while visitors are able to see how most of the Jamaican population live without it being invasive.

One Love Drive Shops

One Love Drive is full of local shops, restaurants, bars, and lodging. All owned and ran by the amazing residents of One Love Village Take a look at our current directory and help support ecotourism and the amazing Jamaican people.

One Love Drive Village

One Love Village is located in the cliffs of Negril. Many employees at local resorts live in the village. This is where they live, eat, and play. Wander up there and join them for a authentic Jamaican Holiday, celebration, or just a chill night.

One Love Youth Group

One of the local ladies in the village volunteers teaches a youth group for the children in the village. For some this is their primary education and for others an after school activity. For more info or to volunteer with the group let us know.

Before You Go...

Mark Twain said it best “travel is fatal to prejudice, bigotry, and narrow-mindedness,”. This experience will test your comfort level and may give you a little culture shock. Lenbert is adamant that this is not an opportunity for handouts to the less fortunate. It’s not encouraged(except treats for the kids). He doesn’t want young kids thinking that they should view tourists as a quick and easy way to get some cash.

It’s community and it’s an opportunity for us to get a sense of those that surround us and support each other. This feels like community tourism at its best. Whether you decide to drink or not, the journey is a busload of fun!
